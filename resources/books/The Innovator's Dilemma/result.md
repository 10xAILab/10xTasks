Title: Disruptive Innovation Explained

Brief description: Clayton Christensen explains disruptive innovation as market democratization, not just incremental improvement. The interview unpacks why incumbents struggle to respond and how theory helps leaders see future threats earlier.

### Key discussion segment 1 (00:00 - 01:33)
Defines disruptive innovation and contrasts it with sustaining innovation using the evolution of computing as a clear example.

### Key discussion segment 2 (01:33 - 03:06)
Introduces the innovator's dilemma: firms are incentivized to serve existing high-margin customers, not emerging low-end markets.

### Key discussion segment 3 (03:06 - 04:39)
Uses automotive competition to show how disruption often starts with simpler offers and then moves up-market over time.

### Key discussion segment 4 (04:39 - 06:12)
Shares the Andy Grove/Intel story to illustrate how applying a framework can reveal strategic responses incumbents miss.

### Key discussion segment 5 (06:12 - 07:45)
Concludes that historical data alone is insufficient for strategy, and that robust theory is essential for future-facing decisions.
