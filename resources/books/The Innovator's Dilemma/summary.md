**Brief description:** Christensen explains why well-managed firms can still fail when disruptive technologies reshape markets. The book shows how incumbent success logic can block adaptation to low-end and new-market disruption.

## Key Insights
- Strong execution against current customers can unintentionally weaken future competitiveness.
- Disruptive innovations often begin in unattractive segments incumbents ignore.
- Organizational structures and incentives must match the uncertainty level of new bets.

## Chapters

### 1. How Great Companies Can Fail
Case studies show that strong incumbent behavior can still produce long-term decline.

### 2. Value Networks and Resource Dependence
Customer demands and existing profit logic shape what firms choose to build.

### 3. Disruptive vs Sustaining Innovation
The core distinction explains why low-performance entrants can later dominate.

### 4. Organizational Capabilities and Disabilities
Processes and values that drive current success can block disruptive execution.

### 5. Managing Disruption
Separate structures, smaller markets, and iterative learning improve disruptive odds.
