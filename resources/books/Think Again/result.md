Title: Adam Grant: Think Again - The Power of Knowing What You Don't Know

Brief description: Adam Grant argues that rethinking is a core competitive skill in careers, leadership, and learning. The talk explains how to challenge certainty without losing conviction, and how to build cultures that update beliefs faster.

### Key discussion segment 1 (00:00 - 04:12)
Opens with why certainty feels good but often blocks learning, setting up rethinking as a deliberate practice.

### Key discussion segment 2 (04:12 - 08:24)
Introduces mental modes (preacher, prosecutor, politician, scientist) to show how people defend identity instead of truth.

### Key discussion segment 3 (08:24 - 12:37)
Explains how strong leaders invite disconfirming evidence and reward thoughtful dissent over performative agreement.

### Key discussion segment 4 (12:37 - 16:49)
Shows personal and organizational examples where updating beliefs early prevents costly strategic drift.

### Key discussion segment 5 (16:49 - 21:01)
Ends with practical methods to institutionalize rethinking through better questions, feedback design, and revision habits.
