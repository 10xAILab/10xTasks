**Brief description:** `Think Again` is about rethinking as a core professional skill rather than a weakness. Grant explains how intellectual humility, curiosity, and evidence-updating improve decisions and relationships.

## Key Insights
- Mental flexibility is a competitive advantage in fast-changing environments.
- Strong reasoning includes the ability to revise beliefs, not just defend them.
- Teams make better decisions when dissent is structured productively.

## Chapters

### 1. A Preacher, a Prosecutor, a Politician, and a Scientist Walk into Your Mind
Grant contrasts defensive mindsets with a scientific mindset that updates with evidence.

### 2. The Armchair Quarterback and the Impostor
He examines overconfidence and impostor dynamics as barriers to accurate self-assessment.

### 3. The Joy of Being Wrong
Treating mistakes as learning signals increases adaptability and long-term performance.

### 4. Good Fights Club
Constructive conflict is framed as a process skill for better decisions.

### 5. Learning to Rethink
Rethinking habits can be embedded in teams through better questions and incentives.
