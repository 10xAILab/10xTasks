Title: Adam Grant: What frogs in hot water can teach us about thinking again | TED

Brief description: Adam Grant explains why people and organizations fail to rethink in time, using the frog metaphor to show how slow change hides risk. He shares practical habits for questioning assumptions before they harden into costly mistakes.

### Key discussion segment 1 (00:00 - 03:11)
Introduces the boiling-frog metaphor as a warning about gradual change and argues that rethinking is a survival skill in fast-moving environments.

### Key discussion segment 2 (03:11 - 06:23)
Explains escalation of commitment and how leaders double down on failing choices to protect identity, status, or sunk costs.

### Key discussion segment 3 (06:23 - 09:35)
Uses personal and career examples to show how identity can lock people into outdated roles and prevent healthy adaptation.

### Key discussion segment 4 (09:35 - 12:47)
Discusses why successful routines can become traps, and why periodic re-evaluation is needed even when performance looks strong.

### Key discussion segment 5 (12:47 - 15:59)
Closes with practical prompts for rethinking, including feedback loops and deliberate challenge of assumptions before decisions scale.
