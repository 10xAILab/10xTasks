Title: Thinking in Bets | Annie Duke | Talks at Google

Brief description: In this Talks at Google session, Annie Duke presents a practical framework for decision-making under uncertainty. She shows how to separate luck from skill and improve outcomes through better process and calibration.

### Key discussion segment 1 (00:00 - 13:55)
Introduces the core premise of Thinking in Bets and why uncertainty requires probability-based decision frameworks.

### Key discussion segment 2 (13:55 - 27:51)
Explains resulting and hindsight bias, emphasizing that outcomes do not reliably reveal whether a decision was good.

### Key discussion segment 3 (27:51 - 41:46)
Discusses confidence calibration and the value of expressing beliefs in ranges and probabilities rather than absolutes.

### Key discussion segment 4 (41:46 - 55:42)
Explores social decision quality, including how to structure disagreement so groups produce better judgments.

### Key discussion segment 5 (55:42 - 69:37)
Outlines practical routines such as decision journals and review loops that turn experience into improved forecasting skill.

### Key discussion segment 6 (69:37 - 83:33)
Concludes with implementation advice for teams and leaders who want repeatable, less ego-driven decision processes.
