Title: Thinking Fast And Slow - Daniel Kahneman

Brief description: This overview of Thinking, Fast and Slow explains the two-system model and the major biases that follow from defaulting to intuition. It focuses on practical implications for everyday decisions, risk, and judgment.

### Key discussion segment 1 (00:00 - 03:12)
Introduces System 1 and System 2, contrasting speed and efficiency with effortful analytical thinking.

### Key discussion segment 2 (03:12 - 06:25)
Explains why cognitive shortcuts are useful but unreliable in complex or high-uncertainty situations.

### Key discussion segment 3 (06:25 - 09:37)
Covers common biases such as availability and anchoring, showing how they distort risk and probability estimates.

### Key discussion segment 4 (09:37 - 12:50)
Examines loss aversion and framing effects that cause inconsistent choices under equivalent information.

### Key discussion segment 5 (12:50 - 16:03)
Concludes with practical guidance to trigger slower thinking when stakes are high or data is ambiguous.
