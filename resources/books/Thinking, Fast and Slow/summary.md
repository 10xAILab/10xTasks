**Brief description:** Kahneman explains two modes of thought: fast, intuitive thinking and slow, analytical reasoning. The book maps common judgment errors and offers a practical vocabulary for improving decisions under uncertainty.

## Key Insights
- System 1 is fast and useful but prone to bias when problems are noisy or complex.
- System 2 can correct errors, but it is effortful and often underused.
- Better decisions come from recognizing context-specific cognitive blind spots.

## Chapters

### 1. Two Systems
Kahneman introduces fast intuition and slow deliberation as interacting decision systems.

### 2. Heuristics and Biases
Mental shortcuts are shown to create predictable errors in judgment.

### 3. Overconfidence
People systematically overestimate certainty in noisy, uncertain environments.

### 4. Choices
Prospect theory explains why gains and losses are evaluated asymmetrically.

### 5. Two Selves
The experiencing self and remembering self can value outcomes differently.
