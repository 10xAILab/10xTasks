Title: Thinking, Fast and Slow | Daniel Kahneman | Talks at Google

Brief description: In this long-form talk, Daniel Kahneman breaks down the two-system model of cognition and how it shapes judgment, attention, and error. He links cognitive biases to real decisions and explains why intuitive confidence is often misleading.

### Key discussion segment 1 (00:00 - 10:23)
Sets context for Kahneman's work and introduces the core distinction between fast intuitive thinking and slow deliberate reasoning.

### Key discussion segment 2 (10:23 - 20:46)
Explores how System 1 and System 2 interact, including the effort cost of attention, self-control, and mentally demanding tasks.

### Key discussion segment 3 (20:46 - 31:10)
Examines common reasoning mistakes and why people accept coherent stories even when evidence quality is weak or incomplete.

### Key discussion segment 4 (31:10 - 41:33)
Details classic bias patterns, especially how intuitive pattern-matching creates confident but fragile judgments in uncertain situations.

### Key discussion segment 5 (41:33 - 51:57)
Connects emotion and framing to decision quality, showing how wording and context can shift choices without changing underlying facts.

### Key discussion segment 6 (51:57 - 62:20)
Wraps with practical implications for better judgment: slow down on high-stakes decisions, check assumptions, and test first impressions.
