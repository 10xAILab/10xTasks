Title: Daniel Kahneman: Thinking Fast vs. Thinking Slow | Inc. Magazine

Brief description: Kahneman contrasts fast and slow thinking for a business audience and shows where intuition helps versus harms. The talk links cognitive science to leadership, decision quality, and organizational error reduction.

### Key discussion segment 1 (00:00 - 01:19)
Frames the two-system model and why leaders often over-trust intuitive judgments in complex environments.

### Key discussion segment 2 (01:19 - 02:38)
Discusses predictable bias patterns that affect hiring, forecasting, and strategic decisions in organizations.

### Key discussion segment 3 (02:38 - 03:57)
Explains how overconfidence and narrative coherence create false certainty, especially when feedback is delayed.

### Key discussion segment 4 (03:57 - 05:16)
Highlights process interventions that reduce bias, including checklists, base rates, and independent estimates.

### Key discussion segment 5 (05:16 - 06:35)
Closes with a practical call to design decision environments that reward accuracy over confidence theater.
