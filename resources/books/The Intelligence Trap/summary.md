**Brief description:** Robson explores why smart people can make severe errors when ego, bias, and overconfidence distort judgment. The book synthesizes cognitive science and practical techniques for improving reasoning quality.

## Key Insights
- Intelligence does not automatically protect against motivated reasoning or blind spots.
- Intellectual humility and cognitive flexibility are practical safeguards against error.
- Better decisions come from process quality, dissent, and reflective checkpoints.

## Chapters

### 1. The Intelligence Paradox
High ability can coexist with poor judgment when reflection and humility are missing.

### 2. Dysrationalia
Reasoning failures are shown as process problems, not pure IQ problems.

### 3. Myside Bias
People defend identity-linked beliefs even when contrary evidence is strong.

### 4. The Curse of Expertise
Deep expertise can harden assumptions and reduce adaptive thinking.

### 5. Wise Reasoning Practices
Perspective-taking and structured reflection improve decision quality over time.
