Title: The Intelligence Trap by David Robson Book Summary

Brief description: This summary explains David Robson's central claim that intelligence alone does not protect against poor judgment. The video highlights cognitive traps that disproportionately affect smart people and offers practical debiasing habits.

### Key discussion segment 1 (00:00 - 01:07)
Introduces the intelligence trap and why high-IQ people can still make serious reasoning errors.

### Key discussion segment 2 (01:07 - 02:15)
Describes motivated reasoning and overconfidence, where intelligence is used to defend beliefs rather than examine them.

### Key discussion segment 3 (02:15 - 03:22)
Shows how expertise can narrow perception, making experienced professionals more vulnerable to blind spots in familiar domains.

### Key discussion segment 4 (03:22 - 04:30)
Outlines practical techniques such as intellectual humility, perspective-taking, and process checks before major decisions.

### Key discussion segment 5 (04:30 - 05:37)
Concludes that wisdom is built through thinking habits, not raw intellect, and must be deliberately practiced over time.
