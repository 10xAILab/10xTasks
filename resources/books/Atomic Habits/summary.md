**Brief description:** `Atomic Habits` explains how small, repeated behaviors create outsized long-term outcomes. Clear presents a practical framework for building good habits and breaking bad ones using identity, environment design, and feedback loops.

## Key Insights
- Tiny behavior changes compound, so consistency matters more than dramatic one-off effort.
- Identity-based habits are more durable than outcome-only goals.
- Environment design is often a stronger lever than willpower.

## Chapters

### 1. The Surprising Power of Atomic Habits
Small habits are shown as compounding units that reshape outcomes over time.

### 2. How Your Habits Shape Your Identity
Behavior change is anchored to "who you are" rather than only "what you want."

### 3. How to Build Better Habits in 4 Simple Steps
The habit loop is framed as cue, craving, response, and reward.

### 4. Make It Obvious
Increase visibility of desired cues and reduce ambiguity around action triggers.

### 5. Make It Attractive
Use temptation bundling and social cues to increase action likelihood.

### 6. Make It Easy
Lower friction, reduce activation energy, and standardize the first step.

### 7. Make It Satisfying
Create immediate reinforcement so habits survive early low-reward phases.

### 8. Advanced Tactics
Tracking, accountability, and recovery strategies help sustain habits under stress.
