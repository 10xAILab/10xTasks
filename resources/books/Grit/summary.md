**Brief description:** `Grit` argues that sustained passion plus deliberate perseverance predicts success better than raw talent alone. Duckworth combines psychology research with stories from sport, education, and business to show how commitment compounds over time.

## Key Insights
- Long-term consistency of effort is often more predictive of outcomes than short bursts of intensity.
- Deliberate practice is uncomfortable by design and is central to skill growth.
- Purpose and hope can be trained, making persistence less dependent on mood.

## Chapters

### 1. Showing Up
Success is reframed as a function of effort and endurance rather than giftedness alone.

### 2. Distracted by Talent
Society tends to overvalue visible talent and undervalue sustained commitment.

### 3. Effort Counts Twice
Skill grows through effort, and achievement grows again when that skill is applied consistently.

### 4. How Gritty Are You?
Duckworth introduces ways to assess and reflect on personal grit levels.

### 5. Grit Grows
Grit can be developed by cultivating interest, practice, purpose, and hope.

### 6. Interest
Long-term motivation strengthens when people work on goals they genuinely care about.

### 7. Practice
Deliberate practice with feedback is presented as the core mechanism for improvement.

### 8. Purpose
Connecting work to contribution beyond self makes persistence more durable.

### 9. Hope
Optimistic explanatory style helps people recover from setbacks and continue.

### 10. Parenting for Grit
Supportive-but-demanding environments are linked to greater resilience and achievement.

### 11. The Playing Fields of Grit
Structured extracurricular commitments can train follow-through over time.

### 12. A Culture of Grit
Groups can encode norms that reward persistence and long-term standards.
