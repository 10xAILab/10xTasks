**Brief description:** The speaker shares a “three gates” framework for picking books that rewire thinking (operator, challenger, fire alarm), then walks through four books—*Fooled by Randomness*, *Think Again*, *Thinking in Bets*, *The Intelligence Trap*—with graduate reads, mental models, and practical drills (luck audit, decision journal, anti-mentor, beginner’s mind).

### Reading for rewiring vs information (00:00 - 01:25)
Contrasts books that inform vs books that change decision machinery; introduces three pre-read gates to filter for real operating-system upgrades.

### The three gates (01:25 - 02:51)
**Operator:** new mental moves, not just new facts. **Challenger:** discomfort and belief disruption. **Fire alarm:** prevents costly confident mistakes—tools that separate confidence from competence.

### Book 1 — randomness and survivorship (02:51 - 04:16)
*Taleb/Fooled by Randomness* via coin-flip thought experiment: many “heroes” are selection artifacts. Personal story on tailwinds vs talent at an AI company; survivorship bias and fragility vs forecasting.

### *Thinking, Fast and Slow* and actions (04:16 - 05:42)
Graduate pairing with Kahneman; **luck vs labor** Friday audit and **wreckage tour** of failed bets with your same strategy.

### Book 2 — unlearning and proficiency prison (05:42 - 07:08)
*Grant/Think Again*: experts can fail more when rules change; Kodak/Blockbuster stories; preacher/prosecutor/politician masks vs scientist mindset; *Innovator’s Dilemma* as advanced lens.

### Scientist, stranger, startup drills (07:08 - 08:33)
Rewrite beliefs as falsifiable hypotheses; explain strategy to an outsider; interview question “how would you destroy this company?” to test scientific thinking.

### Book 3 — decisions as bets (08:33 - 09:59)
*Annie Duke/Thinking in Bets* with personal grad-school bet; **Superforecasting** as graduate read—probabilities and updates, not binary correctness.

### Decision journal (09:59 - 11:24)
30-day practice: confidence %, known/unknowns, evidence that would change odds, and three ways you could be wrong.

### Book 4 — smart people’s failure mode (11:24 - 12:50)
*The Intelligence Trap*: rationalization; **anti-mentor** pre-mortem; **empty cup** naive questions; beginner’s mind anecdote with children in a tunnel.

### Closing: wonder and subscribe (12:50 - 14:15)
Tie books to resetting curiosity; standard YouTube outro.
